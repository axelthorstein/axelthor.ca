All Men Laugh Among The Kind

So you may think that you are right,

In tritely judging foreign souls.

When knowing two things of their life,

He’s too unlike, she’s too unknown.

But know that none are born to right,

And know that none draw earth to moon.

Know all men laugh among the kind,

Know all men come from mother's womb.

Know too,

In pure spite lies sight more askew,

Who keep those close with minds alike,

Where hate can breed fear misconstrued.

Though, I suppose to judge is your right.

Right?

To have abuse be called polite?

And have cold brewing colder night?

To have life’s chore be you alone,

Who dwells on skin and bone and strife.

Perspective’s free for you to own,

But respect is every person's right.

  • Axel Thor
